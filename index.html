🥊 Boxing – The Sweet Science

What is Boxing?

Boxing is a one on one combat sport in which two competitors, wearing padded gloves, attempt to land punches on each other while avoiding being hit. Fighters compete inside a roped square area called a ring.

It's often called "The Sweet Science" since it combines physical power with good understanding of footwork, sharp timing, ring awareness, and mental toughness.

Brief History of Boxing

Boxing dates back to ancient Greece, where it appears in the Olympic Games in 688 BC. The Greek and Roman righters wrapped around their hands and wrists in leather strips called himantes. They fought until one fighter could no longer continue.

Modern Boxing was shaped by the Marquess of Queensberry Rules in 1867, which introduced padded gloves, 3-minute rounds, and wrestling wasn't allowed. These rules became the foundation of the sport we know of today.

Boxing became hugely popular in the 20th century, with legendary fighters like Jack Dempsey, Sugar Ray Robinson, Muhammad Ali, and Mike Tyson becoming household names around the world.

How a Boxing Match Works

  1. Each round lasts 3-minutes, followed by a 1-minute rest.
  2. Fighters try to land clean punches on the opponent's head and body
  3. If a fighter is knocked down, the referee counts to 10.
  4. If the fighter cannot rise before 10, the opponent wins by KO.
  5. If the fight goes all rounds, three judges decide the winner on points.
  6. A match can end by knockout (KO), technical knockout (TKO), judge's decision, or disqualification.

Basic Punches in Boxing

  • Jab: A quick, straight punch with the lead hand. Used to keep distance and set up combinations.
  • Cross: A powerful straight punch with the rear hand. One of the hardest punches in boxing.
  • Hook: A punch thrown in a curved motion, targeting the side of the head or body.
  • Uppercut: An upward punch at the chin or body, usually thrown at close range.

Boxing Equipment

  • Boxing gloves: Padded gloves to protect the hands and the opponent.
  • Hand wraps: Cloth wrapped around the hands and wrists for support.
  • Mouthguard: Protects the teeth and jaw from punches.
  • Headgear: Used in amateur boxing and sparring for head protection.
  • Boxing Shoes: Light shoes designed for grip and ankle support.
  • Heavy bag: Large hanging bar used for power training.
  • Speed bag: Small bag used to improve speed and hand-eye coordination.
